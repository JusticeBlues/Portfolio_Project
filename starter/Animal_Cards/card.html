Wolf

They are social animals hunt in packs and mate for life!

  • Scientific Name: Canis lupus
  • Average Length: 105-150cm (41"-63")
  • Average Lifespan: 6-8 years (may live to 17 in captivity)
  • Habitat: North America, Europe, and Asia

Also known as the grey wolf, they are amazing apex predators. Due to their high intelligence, and social structures they are incredible hunters. It's amazing that their lineage led to todays dogs which are mans best friends.

Jaguar

The word jaguar comes from the indigenous word yaguar, it means he who kills with one leap

  • Scientific Name: Panthera onca
  • Average Length: 1.12-1.85 m (3' 8"-6' 1")
  • Average Lifespan: 12-15 years (may live to 23 in captivity)
  • Habitat: Central and South America

Jaguars are the third largest big cat in the world after the Tiger and Lion. They are fierce predators that will prey upon anything they come across. Unlike other species of cats they are confident swimmers known to prey upon caiman.
